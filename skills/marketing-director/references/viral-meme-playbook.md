# Viral & Meme Marketing Playbook

## Trend Lifecycle — decide entry or skip

| Stage | Signal | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 1. Emergence (0–24h) | Niche communities (X/Reddit/TikTok sounds) using it; no brands yet | Best entry window — ship within 24h |
| 2. Acceleration (24–72h) | Mainstream creators adopt; format variants appear | Still viable if the angle is distinctive |
| 3. Peak (3–7d) | First brands post it; news articles explain it | Enter only with a genuinely subversive twist |
| 4. Decay (7d+) | "Brands ruining the meme" posts appear | Do not enter. A late meme reads as out-of-touch and damages credibility more than silence |

## Brand-Fit Test (all four must pass before shipping)

1. **Voice fit** — could this have plausibly come from the brand's existing tone? A sudden personality change reads as pandering.
2. **Audience overlap** — does the trend's native audience overlap the target audience by more than ~30%? If not, reach is vanity.
3. **Explain test** — does the joke work without a caption explaining it? If it needs explanation, it's already dead.
4. **Screenshot test** — if this is screenshotted and shared out of context in a news article, is it still fine?

## Format Selection

- **Text-only riff** (X/Threads/LinkedIn) — cheapest, fastest; best for stage-1 entry. Ship in under an hour.
- **Image macro / template remix** — needs the current template, not last month's. Verify the template is still live before producing.
- **Short video / sound trend** (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) — highest ceiling, highest effort; only worth it at stage 1–2 with an in-house face or fast creator.
- **Reactive reply / quote-post** — riding someone else's viral moment; near-zero cost, decays in hours, ship immediately or skip.
- **Manufactured viral** (stunts, challenges, giveaways) — the only meme-adjacent format that can be scheduled; plan like a campaign, not a trend-jack.

## Risk Checklist (run before every meme/viral piece)

- **Origin check** — trace where the meme started. Formats originating in hate communities or dark humor about real tragedies are disqualified regardless of current usage.
- **IP** — meme templates using film/TV stills or celebrity likeness carry copyright/publicity risk for commercial accounts; prefer original shots recreating the format, or licensed/owned imagery.
- **Punching direction** — jokes must punch up or sideways (at the brand itself, at competitors carefully, at shared frustrations), never down at customers or groups.
- **Timing collision** — check the news cycle: a playful post during a same-day tragedy or crisis in the brand's market becomes the story.
- **Ratio scenario** — write the most hostile plausible quote-post someone could make. If it lands and there is no good reply, don't post.

## Amplification

Virality is engineered distribution, not luck:

- Seed in 2–3 communities where the format is native before pushing on owned channels.
- Have employees/founder amplify within the first 30 minutes — early velocity determines algorithmic reach.
- Prepare one follow-up in advance: if the post takes off, reply to it with the product tie-in rather than baking the sell into the joke (selling inside the joke kills share rate).

## Measurement

- Primary: shares/reposts (not likes) and non-follower reach percentage.
- A meme post "hit" = 5–10× the account's median reach. One hit per 8–10 attempts is a healthy ratio; optimize the portfolio, not each post.
- Graduate a winning format into the recurring content calendar (moves from the 10% experiment bucket to the 20% scaling bucket).
