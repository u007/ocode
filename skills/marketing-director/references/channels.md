# Channel Prioritization Guide

## Quick-Pick by Objective

| Primary objective | First-priority channels | Support channels |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue (short-term) | Paid search, email/CRM to existing list, retargeting | Affiliate, partnerships |
| Acquisition (new users) | SEO, paid social, viral/meme content, creator collabs | PR, community |
| Activation/retention | Email/CRM lifecycle, in-product, community | Content (how-to), webinars |
| Brand | Short video, PR, sponsorships, distinctive social voice | OOH/events if budget allows |

## Channel Profiles

- **SEO / evergreen content** — compounding, slow (3–6 months to signal); belongs in the 70% bucket always. Wrong choice if the company needs results this quarter and has zero domain authority.
- **Email / CRM** — highest ROI of any owned channel when a list exists; near-zero marginal cost. Prioritize building the list from day one via every other channel.
- **Paid search** — captures existing demand; scales with budget, no compounding. Prioritize when intent keywords exist and unit economics support CAC.
- **Paid social** — creates demand; creative is the targeting now, so it lives or dies on ad-creative volume and testing cadence (expect 10+ variants/week to find winners).
- **Organic social / memes / trend-jacking** — cheap reach lottery with brand-building upside; the 10% experiment bucket's core resident. Requires daily presence and fast approval loops — if legal/brand review takes 3 days, this channel is unavailable to you; fix the approval path first or skip.
- **Short video (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)** — highest organic-reach ceiling of any current channel; needs a recurring face/format, not one-offs. Commit to 3+ posts/week for 8 weeks or don't start.
- **Creator/influencer** — rents trust; prioritize micro-creators (10k–200k) in-niche over celebrities. Judge on saved/shared rates and code redemptions, not follower count.
- **PR** — spiky, credibility-building; prioritize around genuine news (funding, data reports, contrarian research), not feature releases.
- **Community (Discord/Reddit/forums)** — retention and word-of-mouth engine; slow to build, catastrophic to fake. Never astroturf — one exposed sockpuppet costs more than the channel ever earned.
- **Partnerships/co-marketing** — borrow an adjacent audience; cheapest acquisition for B2B. Prioritize when audiences overlap but products don't compete.

## Budget Allocation Heuristics

- Map each channel to the 70-20-10 buckets from SKILL.md; a channel earns promotion to a bigger bucket only with 2+ consecutive weeks beating its kill threshold.
- New company / no list, no SEO: overweight the fast channels (paid, viral, creators) while deliberately seeding the slow compounding ones (SEO, email capture) in parallel — the slow channels are next year's 70%.
- One channel above ~40% of pipeline = concentration risk; start diversifying before it degrades, not after.
